# 📊 Leaderboard Metric & Phased Challenge Specifications

## 1. The Dynamic Giving Scale
To maximize system capital throughput while accommodating traditional corporate legal timelines, the leaderboard tracks and ranks fashion houses based on their active, brand-specific percentage parameters:

- 🟢 **Phase 1: The 1% Baseline Challenge** (Onboarding and initial system validation)
- 🔵 **Phase 2: The 3% Luxury Duels** (Active peer-to-peer brand pairing and real-time public hashtag tracking)
- 🟡 **Phase 3: The 5% Escalation Tracker** (Introduction of localized property preservation and mortgage bridge parameters)
- 🟠 **Phase 4: The 7% Upcycler Match** (Brands actively adopt and cover the impact fees for independent marketplace artisans)
- 🔥 **Phase 5: The 10% Ultimate Tithe** (The maximum luxury giving milestone; funds permanent pre-fab folding home deployments)

## 2. The Crowd-Sourced Social Voting Loop
Everyday citizens and luxury patrons influence the leaderboard rankings for free via decentralized social indexing:
1. **The Ballot:** Users post luxury product content tagged with **#CoutureGivesBack** and **#TheGenerosityRunway**, directly tagging rival houses (e.g., @Dior and @Chanel).
2. **The Tracker:** The protocol's ingestion nodes scan these public tags in real time, converting social engagement into **Community Demand Points** on the public interface.
3. **The Result:** The brand that maintains the highest dynamic giving parameter over a rolling 30-day window claims premium placement on the interface, driving massive conscious consumer acquisition to their storefront.
